Welcome to Pitchtarget!

In this guide we will teach you the basics about how to properly use all the features of the tool. We will show you how to set up your advertising Strategies and monitor their results through our Dashboard.

IMPORTANT

In order for Pitchtarget to function without glitches, Advertising blockers extensions such as Ad Block, must be deactivated. Adblockers may cause malfunctions in some features of the tool, for example while showing the previews of the Ads during the creation process. We will not show any Ad to our users while they use our tool, so don’t worry, you won’t risk to be bothered by banners while you use Pitchtarget!

Settings

Log in

The first step in order to be able to use Pitchtarget is to provide a valid email address for the registration, which do not have to necessarily be the address linked to your Facebook account.
After having signed up into Pitchtarget, the next step would be to login with Facebook and give to the Pitchtarget Facebook App the required permissions. 

In particular, for the tool to properly function, you will need to provide the following permissions:

  • Access to the public info of your profile;
  • Authorize the App to post on your behalf;
  • Choose the audience for posts the App makes on your behalf;
  • Manage your Pages;
  • Publish posts, comments and likes as Pages you manage;
  • Manage your ads;
  • Access your Pages and App insights;
  • Choose whether you want it to send you notifications or not;

After giving the required permissions, you will have to select the Facebook Ad Account you want to use to create, manage and run ads on Facebook. Having an Ad Account is necessary in order to advertise on Facebook. In the case you don’t possess an Ad Account, you will need to create a new one in Facebook.

Conversions Pixel

Conversion Pixels are the elements that allows you to accurately keep track of your Facebook Ads performance. The purpose of Pixels is to track what offsite actions are taken by users interacting with your ads. Pixels allows to identify which ads led to conversions on your website. Conversions may be of various types, for example registrations, sales, page views or add to cart actions.

A Conversion Pixel is a Javascript code snippet that in order to work needs to be copied and pasted between and on the HTML code of the web page where you want to track conversions.

Pitchtarget can create Conversion Pixels for you, for whatever type of conversion you like, but it will not be automatically added into your website. You can do it by yourself following these instructions or you can ask for assistance from an expert (like your webmaster).

Custom Audience Pixel

The Custom Audience Pixel is used to create Website Custom Audiences, which represent any group of customers or prospective customers that express interest in your product on your website. Custom Audiences are created by comparing data about website’s traffic and Facebook’s own data.

Though Facebook Ads can work even without Pixels implementation, they are strongly suggested in order to fully exploit the tool’s tracking and optimization features.

You can view and manage all your Pixels in Pitchtarget’s Pixels section, which can always be reached from the header.

Creating

After you have done all the steps described in the previous section (and eventually implemented the Tracking Pixels) you can start creating your new Facebook advertising Strategy.

Strategy

A Strategy is a sort of repository for Facebook campaigns. It will help you to organize more clearly Facebook campaigns related to different market areas of your business or to the various brands of the company.

We developed the Strategy level in order for our users to better organize their advertising efforts. Strategies are conceived to group together campaigns that are part of the same marketing plan of your business.

All the campaigns in a Strategy refers to a single Facebook Ad Account and Page.

Hence, at this point of the creation process you will just need to select the Ad Account you want to use for the Strategy, specify the Page you want to advertise and give a name to your Strategy.

Campaign

A campaign is a grouping of ad sets which are organized by the same business objective. It helps you optimize and measure the result of your ads.

To enable the campaign to do this, the first thing that is necessary to do is to choose the objective that will correlate every ad set within the campaign.

Objectives

Your advertising objective is what you want people to do when they see your ads. It is very important to have clearly in mind what you want to achieve with your Facebook ads and choose the objective of the campaign correspondingly, because the software will automatically optimize your ads to help you realize your goals in the best way.

The first step in order to set up a campaign is thus to select one out of the following seven objectives:

Boost your posts: the goal is to increase engagement in your posts and get people seeing, liking, commenting on and sharing your Page content. Use this campaign when you need to boost the virality of your posts. You are allowed to choose Video or Photo Posts as creatives with this objective.
Send people to your website: the main focus of this campaign is to drive traffic to your website. These ads will redirect people to your website and you can optionally add a call to action button, for example “Learn More”, to encourage visitors to take that specific action when landing on your website.
Video views: this type of campaign allows you to create ads that embed a video and Facebook will optimize to show them to people most likely to view it. You can choose to add a call to action button, e.g. “Learn More”, “Shop Now” or “Watch More” to invite users to perform that action.
Increase conversions on your website: in order to choose this type of campaign you must install the proper Facebook Conversion Pixel in your website and set it in the Conversion Pixel field when creating the campaign. This type of campaign helps you get more actions done on your website, such as sales or registrations, and tracks your advertising return with a Conversion Pixel. This is the right type of campaign if you are looking to increase your return on investment (ROI) or when you are trying to achieve a lower cost per action (CPA). With this type of campaign you are allowed to choose among Video, Link or Carousel Ad as formats for your creatives.
Get installs of your app: this type of campaign helps you drive more installs for your native mobile apps (iOS or Android). If you want to choose this type of campaign make sure to register your mobile app with Facebook first. You can use pictures or videos in your ads to link people to the app store where they can install your app. If you want to measure exactly the number of app installs, you can integrate your app with the Facebook SDK. This type of campaign also allows you to bid for a cost per action, so you will only pay when people install your app.
Increase engagement in your app: mobile App engagement ads help drive engagement and conversion for your app with ads that take users to a customized location within your app (iOS or Android). For example, a travel app can target its existing users with relevant flight deals and add a "Book Now" call to action button to its Ads. When a person clicks on the button, she will be sent straight into the app to the advertised flight deal. A mobile game, instead, could run an ad targeting its players and challenge them with a "Play Game" call to action by sending them to play a new game level or start a match. If you want to measure the success of your ad, you can integrate your app with the Facebook SDK.
Promote your page: Promoting your page is a great way to increase your number of Page Likes and thus your Fan base. When people like your Page, your posts may appear in their News Feed, along with posts from their friends and family. Promoting your Page might be a good option if you want to engage with your audience or reach new customers.

Once you selected the objective that better suits your needs, you can choose to complete the two remaining fields (which are optional) or simply create the campaign and move to the ad set creation step.

The two optional fields are identified by the labels “Spend Cap” and “Tracking Pixels”.

  • Spend Cap defines a spending cap for the campaign, so you will be sure to not exceed your planned budget. The minimum cap has to be set at 100 €. At the moment it is not possible to use the Spend Cap option for smaller amounts. For sums lower than 100 € the option will return an error and you will have to choose whether to not specify any cap at all or simply set 100 € as your upper limit of expense (we suggest you to always set the Spend Cap to avoid unpleasant surprises that may be caused by accidental oversights).
  • Tracking Pixels field lets you specify which pixels you want to use (if you want to use them) to accurately keep track of your campaign’s results. In order to be able to use pixels, you will first need to create and implement them (as specified in the section 1.2 of this guide). You can select more than one pixel at the same time within a single campaign, in order to be able to break your analysis down in an even more detailed way. We suggest you to use the pixels to fully take advantage of the tools provided by Facebook. However, if for some reason you are not interested in employing them, you will still be able to track the results of your advertising investments (although with less details).

Ad Set

An ad set is a group of ads that share the same daily or lifetime budget, schedule, bid type, bid info, and targeting data. This will help you control the amount you spend on each audience, decide when each audience will see your ads, and see metrics specific to each audience. You can create many ad sets within the same campaign, which is useful if you want to run a campaign with the same objective while targeting different audiences.

Target

The first step to do in order to create an ad set is to specify a target audience. The target audience is the specific group of people within your target market at which your product or the marketing message of the product is aimed.

Thanks to target audiences you can deliver relevant messages to specific people and get the most value from your advert spend by reaching only the people that matter to you.

Pitchtarget allows you to create target audiences specifying for a large number of characteristics, always telling you the estimated reach for the choices you are making. It is up to you to choose how specific you want your target audience to be, according to the knowledge you have of your target market and to the objective of your campaign. In general however, a relatively broad audience may be preferred if your objective is to build brand awareness, while a narrowly defined might be better if you want people to take a specific action on Facebook or on your website or app (such as registrations or purchases).

  • Once you specified an appropriate name for your new target audience, you must specify one or more locations. Locations will be the point from which you can start carving your target. It is possible to set a whole country (or more than one) as target location (e.g., United States, Italy, ecc..) or be more precise and select a specific city. In case you select one or more cities, you will also be able to specify the radius (ranging from 17 km to 80 km) of the areas surrounding the selected locations to be included in the target locations.
  • After you named your target and chose its location, it is already suitable for implementation. However, since it is possible to overlay several targeting options you may want to add some more details to improve target’s accuracy. 
    You can target people based on demographics such as age, gender and languages or on advanced demographics such as: educational background, working experience, relationship status, family status, politics, life events, generation, ethnic affinity, industries and sexual interest.
  • You can also define your ideal target of people by their interests, hobbies and Pages they like on Facebook. 
  • It is also possible to reach people based on their purchasing behavior and device usage, to include or exclude people who are connected to your pages and their friends and Custom Audiences. 
  • Finally, you can choose the Placement of your ads among Desktop News Feed, Right-hand Side Column, Mobile News Feed (iOS, Android or both) and Third Party Apps.
  • If you have doubts while creating a Target, you can “rest” your mouse cursor on the blank cell to be filled with specifics and a little help will appear.
  • Moreover, Pitchtarget automatically stores each audience you create in the Target Templates section, so you can reuse your target audiences whenever you want without having to recreate them from scratch each time. You can draw your audience from the Target Templates by selecting it while in the “New Target” section of the ad set creation process.

The Target Templates can also be accessed anytime by clicking into the “Targets” section in the header of the app. Within this section it is possible to easily create Custom Audiences and Lookalike Audiences starting from your Custom Audiences, to find people who are similar to your current customers.

Budget and Schedule

Once the target has been defined, you need to choose when and how long you want your ads to be shown, the amount of your budget and the bidding modality.

More specifically, concerning the schedule you will have to:

  • Specify the start date (and hour) of the ad set
  • Select whether you want the ad set to run continuously or specify and end date (and hour) for the ad set

While for the budget, you will have to:

  • Specify the maximum amount you want to spend (either daily or lifetime)
  • Choose a bid value for your advertisements or let Facebook automatically pick one for you. In case you want to choose autonomously your own bid value, our suggestion in order to have a successful ad campaign is that you should bid your true value (the highest amount you are willing to spend on your campaign)
  • Select an Optimization Goal. This defines what actions you want the Ad Set to optimize towards. Basically, Facebook will decide which are the most suited persons among your target to see your ads according to the optimization goal you specified for that Ad Set. You will be able to choose among a set of optimization goals pertaining to the objective of your Campaign. If you do not specify an optimization goal, the default one will be the most tightly associated with the Campaign’s objective.
  • Select a Billing Event. This option lets you choose the way you want to pay for your Ads. The available choices for the optimization goals are limited according to the type of campaign you are creating. The chosen optimization goal will in turn constrain the eligible options of billing events. In general, you would be able to choose among impressions or a specific action-related event pertinent to the selected optimization goal (e.g., if the optimization goal is Video Views, you can select impressions or video views as billing event).

Ads

Once the ad set has been created, we can create one or more ads for that ad set. An ad object contains the data necessary to visually display an ad and associate it with a corresponding ad set. Each ad is associated with an ad set and all ads in a set have the same daily or lifetime budget, schedule, and targeting. Creating multiple ads in each ad set will let us optimize their delivery based on variations in images, links, video, text or placements.

There are in total five different types of ads that can be created, however their availability vary according to the objective of the campaign for which the ads are being created.

Link Ad: This kind of ads is designed to send people to important sections of your website or App. It is available for the objectives Send People to your website, Increase App Installs, Increase App Engagement and Increase Conversions on your Website. Link Ads can be optimized to get people to take specific actions on your website or App: conversions, such as signing up for a newsletter or buying a product, as well as App installs and engagement. To create a Link Ad, you will have several editing options: it is possible to insert short Post messages (up to 60 characters long), a link headline (max 13 characters long), a description for the link, a Call to Action button, and of course the URL of the link to be posted. Moreover, since it can happen quite often for some links to look “ugly”, you can choose to insert a brief “Display link” title that will be shown instead of the effective URL of the link you want to post. Finally, for the objectives Increase App Install and App Engagement, it is possible to insert “deep links” that link to a specific location within a mobile app, rather than simply launching it.
Video Ad: Video are one of the most engaging items you can find on a social network. Video Ads are available for the following objectives: Boost Post, Send People to your Website, Video Views, Increase Conversions on your Website and Promote your Page. To create Video Ads you need to upload a video (even very short videos can be a powerful tool to engage your audience), choose a Post title and insert an optional Post message (max 90 characters long). Eventually you can also add a Call to Action button to promote specific actions.
Multiproduct Ad: Also known as “Carousel Ad”, this type of ad is usable for the objectives Send People to your Website or Increase conversions on your Website. It showcases simultaneously up to five images and links within a single ad unit to direct people to specific locations on your website. Since they give the possibility to show many products at the same time they are very effective for conversion campaigns. Moreover, Multiproduct Ads give the chance to unleash your creativity: thanks to their stream of photos\links they allow to tell stories to the customers in a very entertaining and engaging way. To produce a Multiproduct Ad you will need to insert a general Post message (max 90 character long), a general post link and eventually a display link. Then you can edit the single posts content, uploading one picture, a name (max 35 characters long), a brief description (max 30 characters long) a link and an eventual Call to Action button. Even though a Multiproduct Ad can’t exhibit more than five product posts at a time, it is possible to prepare up to ten different product posts. When more than five product posts are created, enabling the Creative Optimization option will let Pitchtarget automatically select and order the best images and links out of those uploaded.
Photo Ad: This type of ad is available only if you selected Boost your Posts as campaign’s objective. The purpose of this kind of campaign is to increase engagement in your Page’s contents, and this can include likes on your post, comments, shares and photo views. Photo Ads are very simple, you just need to upload an interesting picture (or more if you want to perform A\B testing to find out the best performing image) and eventually also a short post message (90 characters max length).
Page Like Ad: This ad format is available only for the objective Promote your Page. Indeed, the purpose of the Page Like Ad is to increase the likes (thus the Fans) of your Page. To create one ad of this type you will need to upload one captivating picture (or more images to do some A\B testing), provide a link headline (max 25 characters long) and a post message (max 90 characters long). Moreover, for desktop placements it is possible to choose a specific view on your Page (About, Timeline, All Activity, likes, Shop, Photos, Contact and Welcome) where people will land when they click on your ad.

Automating

Automatic Boost Post

Facebook gives its users the possibility to promote their posts through the Boost Post feature. Being a kind of ads, boosted posts have more visibility than normal posts. We find this feature to be very useful to improve the performance of businesses on Facebook, because it allows to enhance the effectiveness of their best posts, highlighting the most attractive contents of a Page. While being definitely valuable, Boost Post has a scalability issue. Indeed, while monitoring and setting a boost post is not a difficult or time-expensive task per se, as the number of boosted posts increases so does the cost of investing time in their creation. A relatively simple and quick task then becomes an exhausting set of repetitive and tedious tasks.

At Pitchtarget we found a solution to this problem with our Automatic Boost Posts feature: Automatic Boost Posts does exactly what it is suggested by its name, automating the repetitive tasks of time-consuming manual creation and management of boosted posts. Thanks to the ability to operate on numerous elements simultaneously, each post can be boosted at the same time without having to click through each one of them.

How to use it:

Right after having defined a new Strategy, you are given the possibility to create an ordinary campaign or alternatively, you can choose to create an Automatic Boost Posts campaign.

All you have to do is just set once the rules of an Automatic Boost Posts campaign and then it will automatically operate according to the specified criteria, activating the boosts only when appropriate. Once created, the automation can also be saved for future usage. To create the automation, you first need to specify the name of the new Automatic Boost Posts rule, the post watching time (the number of hours for which the automation will check for each post metric performance), the age of the posts to be included in the campaign and whether to boost all of them simultaneously or only the latest that match the “Trigger”. The next step would be to actually define the “Trigger”, which is the set containing the rules that determine whether a post is eligible to be boosted.Triggers are organized in rule blocks, which share the same operator (“and” or “or”) between the rules contained in them. Rules can be created according to a wide set of parameters (also including the actual text of the posts!) and operators. An example of rule might be: “If the metric 'likes' on post 'xyz' is greater than 100 then boost that post”.

Once the Trigger has been defined, the process becomes almost identical to creating a new campaign: the Target and the Budget have to be set up, and then the automation is ready to go! It is also possible to reuse previously created Targets to further speed up the work flow. Like normal campaigns, each brand new set of Automatic Boost Posts rules is created “in pause”, so that you have the total control of this powerful feature and can decide to launch the automation whenever you like. Each time a new post will be boosted actively, you will be notified via e-mail.

It is also possible to have Pitchtarget automatically stop the Automatic Boost Posts by associating to it a Performance Rule you previously created to determine when it is opportune to stop boosting the post. This option is very useful in our opinion because it allows to save time and money, considering that Facebook charges for the Ads on a pay-as-you-go basis (you pay only for what you consume). For example, if you set an Automatic Boost Posts to reach a specific objective (e.g., 500 likes on that post) within a certain time span, and luckily you happen to reach that objective before the deadline originally set, the Stop Rule will allow you to save the money you would have spent if the Automatic Boost Posts rule had been running even after you had reached your objective.

Performance Rules

Performance Rules is the Pitchtarget’s feature that allows you optimize your resources automatically, according to your own requests. When Performance Rules are appllied, Pitchtarget will monitor the performance of your Campaigns, Ad Sets or Ads and adjust them accordingly, following your instructions. In this way you can save a lot of time that you would have otherwise spent monitoring your Campaigns, while also saving money at the same time thanks to the adjustments produced by the Performance Rules.

The simplest example of how you could apply Performance Rules is creating a “stop rule”.  As you may know, Facebook adopts a pay-as-you-go policy, so that you only pay for what you actually consume (and not necessarily exhaust all your available budget everytime). You may have a specific objective when you launch certain Campaigns, Ad Sets or Ads. For instance, your objective could be achieving a number “x” of one or more specific metrics (likes, shares, impressions, ecc...) for a particular Ad Set. In the case where this objective is achieved before the expected end of the Ad Set’s life cycle, it may be optimal to immediately stop that Ad Set to avoid wasting money by overspending on it. This process can be automatically done by Pitchtarget through the application of a simple Performance Rule that will stop the Ad Set once the threshold determined by your objective is matched. In this way you can reallocate the remaining budget to other operations according to your needs, without having to constantly monitor the Ad Set’s performance.

How to use it:

To access the Performance Rules creation page, you have to click in the section “Rules” in the header of the tool.

If you already created Performance Rules, they will all be showed in a list within this section. By clicking on the “+” icon that will appear on each of the rules’ recap bar while passing the mouse cursor over it, you will be able to associate the considered rule to an element you like within a Strategy.

To create a new rule it is sufficient to click on the “create new Performance Rule” button on the Rules section and you will enter the guided creation procedure. You will have to specify a name for the rule and then create the Trigger in a way similar to the Automatic Boost Posts Trigger. First of all, you will need to specify the subject of your Rule (Campaign, Ad Set, any Ad Set in a Campaign, any Ad in a Campaign or any Ad in an Ad Set) and the time interval of the data to be considered by the rule (from “Today” to “Last 3 months”).
After this, you will have to create the Trigger, which is structured in the same way as the one of Automatic Boost Posts: it is organized in rule blocks, which share the same operator (“and” or “or”) between the rules contained in them. You can create rules combining together about 40 variables (metrics) and 6 operators. The meaning of each variable will be explained by clicking on the “i” icon located aside each variable’s name. 
Once you defined the body of the rule, you will have to specify the action to be taken if the conditions are satisfied. The possible action are:

  • Pause
  • Increase or Decrease Budget
  • Increase or Decrease Bid

For the modifications on the bid or on the budget, it is possible to choose to increase\decrease the value in relative terms (e.g., increase\decrease by 10%) or in absolute terms (e.g., increase\decrease by 10€). In both cases, you will need to specify an upper bound (maximum) for each increase action and a lower bound (minimum) for each decrease action. Once the action is specified, you will have to select the “recipient” of that action. You can choose among Campaign, Ad, any Ad in a Campaign, any Ad Set in a Campaign, any matching Ad Set, any matching Ad.

After having determined what action to undertake and where to perform it, you will need to specify (under the heading “Apply this rule every”) the number of hours you want the rule to check whether the conditions you set are matched. Finally, you will need to provide an e-mail address that will be used to notify you whenever an automation starts.

It is possible to associate one or more Performance Rules to a Campaign, Ad Set or to an Automatic Boost Posts. To do it, you need to go to the strategy list in “Strategies” and select the “edit strategy” icon of the Strategy containing the Campaigns, Ad Set or Automatic Boost Posts to which you want to apply your Performance Rules (the edit icon will appear when you pass your mouse cursor over the Strategy recap bar). Once inside the edit section of the Strategy, to associate a previously created Performance Rule to a Campaign you need to click on the “+” icon that will appear while you pass your mouse cursor over the Campaign or Automatic Boost Posts recap bar, and select the rules you want to attach to the Campaign. To associate a Performance Rule to an Automatic Boost Posts, once inside the edit section of the Strategy containing the interested element, select the edit icon of the Automatic Boost Posts you want to associate to the rule and then click on the “Associate Performance Rule” button at the bottom of the Trigger creation page. This action can be also performed at the same time of the Automatic Boost Posts creation, provided that the Performance Rule already exists.

Launching

Each time an ad is created, it undergoes Facebook’s own revision for compliance.
If the ad is approved, then it is allowed to be published.

However, remember that the ads will not be active until you say so: every Strategy and Campaign is created “in pause”, and to activate it you need to “turn on the switch” of the Campaign by clicking on it (the switch is illustrated in the picture). In this way, while Facebook does its review, you can do your own to check whether everything is all right. If you want the Campaign to go live as soon as Facebook officially gives its approval, you just need to turn on the Campaign’s switch right after you finish creating the last ad.

Generally, at the end of each “create new Campaign” process you will be facing a “recap screen” in which you will be given the possibility to turn on the switch of the Campaign and effectively go live with your ads or to further edit elements of the Campaign. Once you press the “done” button you will be automatically redirected to the Dashboard.

Monitoring

The Dashboard

Once you finished creating the ads and the campaign has been launched and is active, it is possible to start monitoring its performance through Pitchtarget’s Dashboard.

The Dashboard can be analyzed at 3 hierarchical levels: Strategy, Campaign and Ad Set.

By default the Strategy level Dashboard will be the first shown right after you finish editing your campaign.

At the Strategy level it is possible to obtain an overview of how the various Campaigns are performing.

At the Campaign level it is possible to see how the various Ad Sets of a Campaign are performing.

At the Ad Set level it is possible to analyse the performance of the single Ads belonging to the selected Ad Set.

Moreover, you can enter the Dashboard through the Strategy lists accessible by clicking “Strategies” in Pitchtarget’s header menu. Here you will find the list of your strategies, which can be filtered to show only the active, the paused or all the strategies you created. The recap bar of each strategy will give you an overview of the performance of the strategies. The recap bar is structured to show the status and the period of activity of a Strategy, the number of Campaigns included in the Strategy, the total budget and the total spend of the whole Strategy, plus the metrics Reach, CPM, CPC and CTR ( which are explained in the “Metrics” section below). In addition, each Strategy recap bar can be expanded\collapsed to show\hide more detailed information, such as the Page used for the Strategy, the number of Campaign in progress and those already ended, and the overall number of Ad Sets and Ads.

Navigation

To get to a “deeper” level of the Dashboard it is sufficient to click on the right-pointing arrow that will appear as the last icon of the hidden menu in the Strategy recap bar or in the Campaign\Ad Set metrics bar of the element you want to analyse. The hidden menu will appear each time you let the mouse cursor pass over the metrics bar (see figure). While in the Campaign or Ad Set level of the Dashboard, it is possible to quickly reach specific “hierarchically higher” levels using also the breadcrumb trail located above the charts (see figure).

Charts

Pitchtarget allows you to visualize the charts of every element of your Strategy you like (Campaigns, Ad Sets or Ads) for every metric you want. Pitchtarget will show the charts for two metrics at a time. You can select a different metric at anytime by deselecting the one you don’t want to analyse anymore and select the new one.

You can select or deselect the number of elements you want to compare on the charts at anytime. For example, if you want to analyse the graphs of only 2 out of the 4 Campaigns present in a Strategy, you just need to check the boxes that will appear in the left end of metrics bar of the element you want to select while you pass your mouse cursor over it. In this way, the Dashboard will display the charts only for the selected elements.

The color shown at the left end of each element’s metrics bar work as the legend that will allow you to identify each element on the charts.

At the Strategy level Dashboard, Pitchtarget will show the line charts of the selected metrics for the considered Campaigns. At the Campaign and Ad Set levels the Dashboard will show, additionally to the line charts, a further breakdown of the metric analysis by placement. The percentages relative to the results of the selected metrics on the basis of their placements (News Feed, Mobile, or Right-hand Column) will be shown through a donut chart or a list (according to the metric) which will be located aside of the line chart.

NOTE:

The heading “non_feed” in the donut charts identifies the metrics values registered with as placement the Right-hand Column Ads on desktop computers. These are different from those under the heading “right_hand”, which represent the placement in the Right-hand Column Ads on home page for desktop computers. Thus, “right_hand” will refer to Ads shown in the Right-hand Column while the user is on the News Feed (Facebook’s home page), while “non_feed” will refer to Ads shown in the Right-hand Column while the user was exploring other Pages within Facebook (different from the News Feed).

At the Strategy level on the top left corner of the Dashboard screen will be shown the Page whose performance you are analyzing.

CAREFUL:

Each metric will show the value referred to the selected Date Range, showed just above the charts. It is possible to reset the dates considered at anytime, or choose the date range you prefer. So, be sure that you are aware of the period of the data you are monitoring.

Just above the charts it will be shown the time slot according to which the data are being displayed. By clicking over the dates, it will be possible to set the Date Range that better suits your needs. You can also quickly reset the dates back to their original value by clicking on the “Reset Dates” button located aside the Date Range.

The X-axis of the charts will represent time, while on the Y-axis will represent the unit of measurement of the selected metric. On the right end of the graphs, the maximum and minimum amount of the metrics will be shown.

Metrics

With Pitchtarget it is possible to analyse the performance of your advertising strategy through 48 different metrics, organized in 9 different categories including a “General” category that contains those which can give the clearest first glance of the overall performance.

The metrics will be the same across different Campaign objectives, exception made for the first element of the metrics bar, which will correspond to the most relevant Key Performance Indicator (KPI) according to the specific objective selected for the campaign (e.g., if you selected “Send people to your website” as objective for a campaign, the KPI will be the number of link clicks obtained with that campaign ).

Each metric is referred to a specific element (nominally Strategy, Campaign, Ad Set or Ad) according to the Dashboard level you are analysing. Here you can find the list of all the metrics of the Dashboard and their meanings, organized by category:

General:

  • Spend: the amount of money actually spent.
  • Reach: the number of unique people who received impressions.
  • Impressions: the number of times an ad is displayed. People may see multiple impressions of the same ad.
  • Frequency: the average impressions per person.
  • Clicks: every click performed on an ad.
  • CTR: or Click-Through Rate, the number of clicks for every impression.

General Costs:

  • Spend: same as in “General” above.
  • Reach: same as in “General” above.
  • CPM: or Cost per mille, is the average cost for every thousand impressions.
  • CPC: or Cost per Click, is the average cost for every click obtained.
  • CPA: or Cost per Action, is the average cost for every action (like, comment, share, app install, ecc.) obtained.

Engagement:

  • Likes: the number of likes obtained.
  • Cost per Like: the average cost per like obtained.
  • Actions: the number of actions (likes, shares, comments, app installs, ecc.) produced.
  • Cost per Action: the average cost per action produced.
  • Shares: the number of shares obtained.
  • Comment: the number of comments obtained.

Website:

  • Link Clicks: the number of link clicks obtained.
  • Cost per Link Click: the average cost per link click obtained.
  • Conversions*: the number of conversions (i.e., customer-completed actions, like purchases or adding to a cart on a website) obtained from your ads.
  • Cost per Conversion*: the average cost per conversion obtained.
  • Conversions Value*: the total value of the conversions tracked on your website after people viewed or clicked on your ads.
  • ROAS*: or Return On Advertising Spending, represents the dollars earned per dollars spent on the corresponding advertising. It is obtained dividing the revenue derived from the ad source by the cost of that ad source.

*these metrics require an appropriate tracking pixel to be installed in your website to work.

App:

  • App Actions: the number of actions, including app installs, credit spends and uses as a result of your ads.
  • Cost per App Action: the average cost per app action obtained.
  • App Installs: the number of app installs obtained.
  • Cost per Install: the average cost per app install.

Video:

  • Video Views: the number of times your videos were viewed for 3 seconds or more.
  • Cost per Video View: the average cost per video view.
  • AVG % Watched: the average percentage of your video that people viewed.
  • AVG Sec. Watched: the average amount of seconds people spent viewing your video.
  • Complete Video Views: the number of times your video was viewed to 100% of its length.

The following categories of metrics are useful only if preceded by the install of the appropriate tracking pixel on your website.

Tracking View:

  • Key Page Views: the number of times a key page was viewed on your website as a result of your ad.
  • Key Page View Cost: the average cost per key page view obtained.
  • Key Page View Actions Value: the total value returned when a key page was viewed on your website as a result of your ad.
  • Other: the number of other conversions on your website that happened as a result of your ad.
  • Other Cost: the average cost for each time someone took another action on your website as a result of your ad.
  • Other Conversion Actions Value: the total value returned from other conversions on your website as a result of your ad.

Tracking Lead:

  • Lead: the total number of leads obtained.
  • Lead Cost: the average cost per lead obtained.
  • Lead Actions Value: the total value returned from acquiring new leads on your website as a result of your ad.
  • Registrations: the number of registrations tracked on your website after people viewed or clicked on your ad.
  • Registration Cost: the average cost per registration obtained.
  • Registration Actions Value: the total value returned from completed registration conversions on your website as a result of your ad.

Tracking Purchase:

  • Add to Cart: the number of times something was added to a shopping cart on your website as a result of your ad.
  • Add to Cart Cost: the average cost per add to cart action obtained.
  • Add to Cart Actions Value: the total value returned when items were added to a cart on your website as a result of your ad.
  • Checkouts: the number of checkout initiations tracked on your website after people viewed or clicked on your ad.
  • Checkout Cost: the cost per checkout action obtained.
  • Checkouts Actions Value: the total value returned from checkout conversions on your website as a result of your ad.

Editing

The Metrics Bar

Every element at every level on the dashboard has its own metrics bar that can be expanded or collapsed to show or hide additional information about that element, just like the recap bar of the “Strategies” section.  In particular, at the Ad Set level Dashboard, by expanding the metrics bar of an Ad, it is possible to get a preview of the contents of the Ad, like pictures, videos, text and call to actions. This in our opinion is a really useful feature that allow to save a lot of time while monitoring the Campaign’s performance.

As said in the Navigation part, letting pass the mouse cursor over an element’s metrics bar will make appear the hidden menu containing the right-pointing arrow icon that enables to reach deeper levels of the Dashboard. Together with the right-pointing arrow, there will appear other three (two for the Ad Set level Dashboard) icons that will enable to perform different action according to the level of the Dashboard you are in:

While you are in the “Strategies” section and you are facing the lists of the various Strategy recap bars, the hidden menu of each recap bar will show you four icons that, in order, will allow you to: add a new Campaign to the selected Strategy; edit the Campaigns of the Strategy (eventually adding an Automatic Boost Posts or associating a Performance Rule to it); delete the whole Strategy; enter the Dashboard at the Strategy level.  

  • At the Strategy level the hidden menu of each metrics bar will show four icons that, in order, will allow you to: export the data of the selected Campaign as a CSV file; add an Ad Set to the selected Campaign; edit the elements of the Campaign; reach the Campaign level Dashboard.
  • At the Campaign level the hidden menu of each metrics bar will show four icons that, in order, will allow you to: export the data of the selected Ad Set as a CSV file; add an Ad to the selected Ad Set; edit the Ad Set; reach the Ad Set level Dashboard.
  • At the Ad Set level hidden menu of each metrics bar will show three icons that, in order, will allow you to: export the data of the selected Ad as a CSV file; get a preview of what the selected Ad will look like in the different placements; edit the contents of the selected Ad.
  • It is also possible to quickly activate or deactivate every Campaign, Ad Set or Ad at anytime in the Dashboard. You just need to turn on or off the switch which is located in the metrics bar under the element’s name. Moreover, in the metrics bar of the Ads it is possible to monitor the 1 to 10 relevance score of each single Ad, that Facebook will provide after the Ad is served more than 500 times.

Sign Out and Change Account

To sign out from Pitchtarget, it is sufficient to click on the “Sign Out” button located on the right corner of the tool’s header.

If you are using Pitchtarget with more than one Ad Account, you can quickly switch between your accounts. By clicking on the name of the Ad Account you are currently using, located on the right corner of the tool’s header, a drop-down list containing all the account registered on Pitchtarget will appear. To start using one of these account you just need to click onto the one you want to use and you will be brought to that account’s Strategy list (the section “Strategies” of the tool’s header).

To refresh your account list you can press the refresh icon located aside of your account’s name on the tool’s header.
